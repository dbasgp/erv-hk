THC 恆溫恆濕機 vs 冷氣 + 抽濕機。 THC climate control vs split AC + dehumidifier.

傳統做法:一台冷氣負責溫度、一台抽濕機負責濕度、再加一台加濕器補濕。DBA THC 系列分體式恆溫恆濕機把四件事整合到一個系統 — 但要不要選它,還是要看實際需求。 The traditional answer: one AC for temperature, one dehumidifier for humidity, a humidifier for dry months. The DBA THC range packs all four jobs into one split-system chassis — but whether it suits your room depends on what you actually need.

THC 是甚麼?

DBA-THC 系列是分體式機型,室內機(吊裝於假天花)配室外機(壓縮機冷凝器組合)。可同時處理:

  • 製冷 — 與冷氣機相同,降低溫度
  • PTC 暖氣 — 透過陶瓷加熱元件提供低溫天氣加熱
  • 抽濕 — 獨立於溫度控制,維持目標 RH
  • 加濕 — 經內置加濕模組,於乾燥季節(冬季)補濕

用一個 LCD 控制面板,設定目標溫度與目標 RH,機身自動決定何時製冷/加熱/抽濕/加濕。三個機型:THC35(350 平方呎)、THC50(500 平方呎)、THC66(650 平方呎)。

「冷氣 + 抽濕機」的傳統做法

大部份香港住宅用以下組合:

  • 分體式冷氣 — 製冷時順帶抽掉一些濕氣,但 SHR 約 0.75,即冷氣每移走 1 kW 熱量,只有 0.25 kW 是潛熱(濕氣)
  • 移動或天花式抽濕機 — 補足濕度控制
  • 冬天天氣乾燥時,額外用加濕器

三件套各自運作,各自有控制器,各自佔位。整體佔空間多,線材多,風喉多,但每件成本較低。

THC 一機合一的好處

  • 單一控制邏輯 — 不會發生冷氣設定 23°C / 抽濕機設定 50%RH 互相抗衡的情況
  • 佔位較少 — 假天花內只一台機,風喉一套
  • 四季合用 — 香港 12 月至 2 月空氣可以乾到 30% RH(室內配合冷氣後更低),THC 自帶加濕功能解決皮膚乾裂、家具開裂問題
  • 較簡潔的設計感 — 對講求設計的住宅、會所、精品酒店,單一系統美觀

幾時應該繼續用傳統組合?

  • 已安裝冷氣,只需追加抽濕 — 加裝 UTC 或 GEC 天花式抽濕機即可,改成 THC 需重做雪種喉
  • 每個房間獨立溫控需求不同 — THC 一機服務 350-650 平方呎範圍,大宅可能需多台,反而組合方式更靈活
  • 預算考慮 — THC 機身與安裝費用比 UTC + 普通分體冷氣的組合貴 30-50%
  • 樓底太淺 — THC 機身較大,需更深的假天花空間

能源效益比較

單純比較「冷氣只做冷氣 vs THC 做所有事」並不公平,因為功能不同。較公平比較:

  • 傳統組合(冷氣 + 抽濕機 + 加濕器):總能耗較低,但三件套各自於自身效率區間運作
  • THC 一體機:總能耗略高,但因為單一控制,避免冷氣與抽濕機抗衡,實際運轉時間更短

實測下,中型住宅(500 呎)使用 THC50 比同等三件套組合,年度電費差距約 ±10%,並非決定性因素。決定性因素是空間與設計需求。

簡單建議

新建住宅、精品設計項目、會所、需要冬季加濕功能 → THC 系列。已有冷氣、預算敏感、空間受限 → 冷氣 + UTC/GEC 抽濕機組合。

What is the THC range?

The DBA-THC range is a split-system: a ceiling-mounted indoor unit paired with an outdoor compressor / condenser. It handles four jobs:

  • Cooling — like an air conditioner
  • PTC heating — ceramic-element heat for cold spells
  • Dehumidification — independent of temperature, holds target RH
  • Humidification — built-in module adds moisture in dry months

One LCD panel sets target temperature and target RH; the unit decides when to cool, heat, dehumidify, or humidify. Three sizes: THC35 (350 sq ft), THC50 (500 sq ft), THC66 (650 sq ft).

The traditional "AC + dehumidifier" approach

Most HK homes use:

  • Split AC — cools, with incidental dehumidification at SHR ≈ 0.75 (only 25% of the work is latent)
  • Mobile or ceiling dehumidifier — handles humidity properly
  • Plug-in humidifier for dry winter months

Three boxes, three controllers, three positions. More wiring, more ducting, more space — but lower individual cost.

What the THC integration gains you

  • One control logic — no fight between the AC at 23°C and the dehumidifier at 50% RH
  • Less plenum real estate — one unit, one duct system
  • Year-round coverage — HK winters can hit 30% RH outdoors and lower with AC running; the THC humidifies to keep your skin and furniture from drying out
  • Cleaner design — for high-end residential, clubhouses, and boutique hotels, one ceiling system is visually quieter

When the traditional combo still wins

  • Existing AC, just adding dehumidification — bolting on a UTC or GEC ceiling unit is cheaper than re-piping for THC
  • Multiple rooms with different temperature needs — THC serves 350–650 sq ft per unit; larger homes need several, and a mixed approach can be more flexible
  • Budget-constrained — THC capex and install runs 30–50% above a UTC + standard split combo
  • Tight ceiling voids — THC chassis is deeper than a UTC; not every plenum fits

Energy comparison

Direct head-to-head is unfair because the systems do different work. A fairer comparison:

  • Traditional combo (AC + dehumidifier + humidifier): lower individual energy, but each device sits in its own efficiency band
  • THC integrated: slightly higher headline draw, but one control loop avoids the AC-vs-dehumidifier tug-of-war, and total runtime is shorter

In a typical 500 sq ft HK flat, THC50 vs the equivalent three-box combo comes out within ±10% on annual energy. The decisive factor is rarely energy — it is space and design.

The short version

New-build, design-led, clubhouse, or you want winter humidification → THC range. Existing AC, budget-sensitive, tight plenum → keep the AC and add a UTC/GEC ceiling dehumidifier.
