風喉設計Ducting · 7 分鐘閱讀7 min read

香港假天花的風喉設計。 Ducting design inside HK plenums.

香港住宅假天花淨高常見只有 250-350mm,需要塞風喉、雪種喉、噴淋、電纜、再加抽濕機本身 — 風喉設計變成毫米級的取捨遊戲。 HK residential plenums are commonly 250–350 mm clear. Add ductwork, refrigerant pipes, sprinkler runs, cabling, and the dehumidifier itself, and ducting design becomes a millimetre-by-millimetre trade-off.

外靜壓是甚麼?

每部天花式抽濕機都有一個「外靜壓限值」(External Static Pressure, ESP) — 即風機可承受的風喉系統阻力。超過這個數值,風量會明顯下降,抽濕能力跟住下降。DBA 機型的 ESP:

  • UTC20 / 68 / 120 — 約 30-40 Pa
  • GEC V (30/50/70/110) — 約 60-80 Pa
  • GEC68LD-HP / 145LD-HP — 約 80-120 Pa
  • GEC280LD 以上 — 約 150 Pa

香港住宅 UTC 系列大部份只跑 1-2 個出風口,通常不會接近上限。但會所、酒店大廳等使用 GEC V 或商用 GEC 鋪 4-6 個出風口的項目,就要認真計算。

香港常用風喉類型

  • 絕緣鋁箔軟管 — 最常用。安裝容易,但每米靜壓損失約 1.5-2.5 Pa
  • 螺旋鍍鋅鋼風喉 — 商用項目較多,阻力低(每米 0.5-1.0 Pa),但需更多假天花空間及人手安裝
  • 方形鋼風喉 — 大型商用,可貼樓底,但需大量裁切及保溫

每個元件的靜壓損失

計算總靜壓的方法 — 把所有元件累加:

  • 軟管直管:每米 1.5-2.5 Pa
  • 軟管 90° 急彎:每個 10-15 Pa
  • 鋼喉 90° 大彎:每個 3-5 Pa
  • 送風口(4 向擴散器):5-15 Pa
  • 回風口 + 濾網(乾淨):15-25 Pa
  • 回風口 + 濾網(用了半年):40-60 Pa

設計時應以「濾網變髒」的數字計算,而非「新濾網」 — 否則安裝後 6 個月,風量就會跌得很明顯。

香港假天花的實際處理

常見處理策略:

  • 單口直送 — 機身與唯一送風口距離 1-2 米,UTC20 / UTC68 適用。最簡單,靜壓損失最小。
  • 單口短軟管 + 4 向擴散器 — 機身藏於走廊上方,軟管 2-3 米送到客廳中央。最普及的設計。
  • 分支結構 — 一台 GEC V 70 服務客廳 + 主臥 + 書房。從機身出風口拉一條主管,再分支至各房。需仔細平衡各支管的長度與風口大小。

保溫

香港夏季假天花溫度可達 30-35°C,而風喉內送風溫度約 18-22°C。如不保溫,風喉外壁會凝水,長時間下會浸壞旁邊的木板、石膏板。所有送風段風喉必須保溫;香港最常用是預先保溫的鋁箔軟管,內裡有 25mm 玻璃棉。

70-90% 原則

設計時讓總靜壓計算結果落在機型 ESP 的 70-90% 範圍。低於 70% 浪費了部份機型成本;超過 90% 沒有預留濾網老化空間。

What is external static pressure?

Every ceiling dehumidifier has a maximum external static pressure (ESP) — the fan's tolerance for duct resistance. Exceed it and airflow falls, capacity falls. DBA's ESP ratings:

  • UTC20 / 68 / 120 — 30–40 Pa
  • GEC V (30/50/70/110) — 60–80 Pa
  • GEC68LD-HP / 145LD-HP — 80–120 Pa
  • GEC280LD and up — 150 Pa

Most HK residential UTC installs only run one or two grilles, so ESP is rarely an issue. Clubhouse and hotel lobby projects using GEC V or commercial GEC with 4–6 grilles need real calculation.

Common duct types in HK

  • Insulated aluminium-foil flex: the workhorse. Easy install but loses 1.5–2.5 Pa per metre.
  • Spiral galvanised rigid: common in commercial. Lower loss (0.5–1.0 Pa/m) but needs more plenum space and skilled installation.
  • Rectangular sheet metal: large commercial only. Can hug the slab but requires fabrication and added insulation.

Pressure losses per element

Total static = sum of all losses:

  • Flex straight: 1.5–2.5 Pa/m
  • Flex 90° sharp bend: 10–15 Pa each
  • Rigid 90° long-radius bend: 3–5 Pa each
  • Supply grille (4-way diffuser): 5–15 Pa
  • Return grille + clean filter: 15–25 Pa
  • Return grille + 6-month-old filter: 40–60 Pa

Always design with the dirty filter number, not the clean one — otherwise airflow noticeably drops six months after install.

HK plenum strategies

  • Direct single supply: unit 1–2 m from the only supply grille. Works for UTC20 / UTC68. Simplest, lowest loss.
  • Short flex + 4-way diffuser: unit hidden above a corridor, 2–3 m of flex reaches a diffuser in the living room. Most popular HK pattern.
  • Branched: one GEC V 70 feeding living + main bedroom + study. Trunk from the unit, branches to each zone. Branch lengths and grille sizes need balancing.

Insulation

HK summer plenums hit 30–35°C; supply air is 18–22°C. Without insulation the duct skin condenses, eventually staining adjacent timber or plasterboard. All supply ducts must be insulated; the standard HK choice is pre-insulated foil flex with a 25 mm glass-wool core.

The 70–90% rule

Aim for total static between 70% and 90% of the unit's ESP. Under 70% means oversized fan capacity. Over 90% leaves no margin for filter loading.
